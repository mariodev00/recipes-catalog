Trader Joe's Lobster Pasta

If there is anything more decadent and satisfying to dig into than a steaming bowl of lobster pasta, I haven't found it yet. It's one of my favorite summer meals to treat myself to when the mood strikes, though this is a rarity because of how time-consuming and expensive all things seafood can be.
So when our Allrecipes team caught wind of a quick, easy, and affordable hack to make restaurant-level lobster pasta a weeknight reality, we knew we struck recipe gold. Possibly the best part of this dish is that it only requires a handful of ingredients—all from Trader Joe's for around $6 a person. We (finally) had to see what all the fuss was about.

How to Make Trader Joe's Lobster Pasta

To recreate it for yourself, sauté about half a diced onion and a few chopped garlic cloves in olive oil until soft, and then add in a hefty handful of halved cherry tomatoes. Cook that down until soft and fragrant, and season with salt and pepper before adding in the bag of (thawed and rinsed) lobster-copycat langostino meat and the container of TJ's lobster bisque.
Once heated through, brighten it up as Nicole does with crushed red pepper, Parmesan cheese, lemon zest, and lemon juice to taste. Finally, add in a pound of your favorite pasta shape. Stir everything together to let the starchy pasta mix with the creamy sauce, then finish it all off with a sprinkling of fresh parsley and some more Parmesan. Now dinner perfection is yours.
